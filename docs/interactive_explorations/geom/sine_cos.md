# Deriving the Sine Graph from the Unit Circle

This exploration investigates how the graphs of the sine functions arise from the unit circle.

Increase the size of the angle \(s\) using the first button on the graph. When you think you have an idea of what the graph of \(y=\sin x\) looks like, click on the second button to reveal the graph.

<div id="hint1" class="hint" style="display:none; margin-top:10px;">
  <b>THINK!</b> <br>

  1. What happens to the \(y\)-coordinate of point \(P\) as the angle increases from \(0\) to \(\dfrac\pi2\)? <br><br>
  
  2. What value of \(s\) gives the highest possible \(y\)-coordinate? How does this relate to the graph of \(y=\sin x\)? <br><br>

  3. What is the <b>amplitude</b> of the graph of \(y=\sin x\)? Remember, the amplitude is the distance between the middle value and the highest or lowest point on the graph. <br><br>

  4. What is the <b> period </b> of the graph of \(y=\sin x\)? Remember, the period is the length (measured horizontally) of one complete cycle in a periodic graph.
