# Modelling Motion on a Wheel with Trigonometric Functions

This exploration investigates how the graphs of the sine and cosine functions can be used to model the path of a seat on a ferris wheel.

The screen below shows a rider on a ferris wheel, represented by the purple point. The moving point on the right shows the person's height above the ground at different points in time.

  3. What might the <b> amplitude </b> of the graph represent in this case? <br>

  4. What is the relationship between the <b> principal axis </b> of the graph and the ferris wheel? <br>

  5. How do we decide on an appropriate function \(y=A\sin(B(x-C))+D\) or \(y=A\cos(B(x-C))+D\), and the values of \(A,\;B,\;C\) and \(D\)?
